Green onions

Product Description

  • Soil: Green onions grow best in well-drained, loamy or sandy loam soil rich in organic matter, with a pH range of 6.0–7.0. The soil should be loose and well-aerated to allow for easy root growth. Adding compost or well-decomposed manure helps improve soil fertility.
  • Climate: Green onions thrive in cooler climates with temperatures between 13–24°C. They can tolerate a range of temperatures, but they grow best in moderate climates, as extreme heat can cause them to bolt (flower prematurely).
  • Fertilizer: Green onions are light feeders, but applying a balanced organic fertilizer or compost at planting time promotes strong growth. Additional light feeding with a nitrogen-rich fertilizer can be beneficial during growth, especially if the soil is not very fertile.
  • Planting: Green onions can be grown from seeds, transplants, or even kitchen scraps. To grow from seeds, sow them about 1–2 cm deep, spacing them 10–15 cm apart in rows. If using transplants or scraps (cuttings), plant them 1–2 cm deep and space them similarly. Green onions can be grown in containers or directly in the ground.
  • Watering: Green onions require consistent moisture for proper growth. Water regularly, especially during dry periods, to maintain soil moisture. However, avoid waterlogging, as this can lead to root rot. Drip irrigation is ideal, as it provides steady moisture without wetting the foliage.
  • Weeding: Weeding is important in the early stages of growth to reduce competition for nutrients. Once established, the green onions' dense foliage helps to suppress weed growth.
  • Thinning: If seedlings are too crowded, thin them out to about 5–7 cm apart to allow enough space for the onions to grow properly. This will help reduce competition for nutrients and water.
  • Harvesting: Green onions are typically ready for harvest in 8–12 weeks from sowing, depending on the variety and growing conditions. Harvest when the stems are about 20–30 cm tall. You can pull the entire plant or cut off the outer stalks, leaving the smaller ones to continue growing.
  • Post-harvest Management: Green onions are best used fresh, but they can be stored in the refrigerator for up to a week in a damp paper towel or sealed container. Alternatively, they can be regrown by placing the roots in a glass of water, where new shoots will emerge.
