Water Chestnut

Product Description

  • Soil: Water chestnuts grow best in clay or silty soils with good organic content. They require a waterlogged or submerged environment with a pH range of 6.5–7.5.
  • Climate: Warm, humid climates with temperatures between 20–30°C are ideal for water chestnuts. They require flooded fields or ponds for growth, but stagnant water should be avoided.
  • Fertilizer: Apply organic fertilizers, such as compost or well-decomposed manure, at the beginning of the growing season. Supplement with nitrogen-based fertilizers every 15–20 days for optimal growth.
  • Planting: Prepare the field by ploughing and leveling it to maintain an even water level. Plant the corms (tubers) in shallow, flooded areas with spacing of about 30–40 cm between plants to allow for proper growth.
  • Transplanting: Plant water chestnut corms directly in the prepared waterlogged field, usually at the beginning of the rainy season or when temperatures are warm enough.
  • Watering: Maintain a consistent water level of about 15–20 cm above the soil surface throughout the growing season to provide the right conditions for water chestnut growth.
  • Weeding: Begin weeding as soon as plants are established to prevent competition for nutrients. Repeat as necessary throughout the season.
  • Harvesting: Water chestnuts are typically ready for harvest 6–7 months after planting. Harvest when the plants begin to die back, and the nuts are firm and mature.
  • Post-harvest Management: Rinse the harvested water chestnuts to remove soil and debris. Store in a cool, dry place to extend freshness and prevent mold or decay.
