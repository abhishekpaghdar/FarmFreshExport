Rice

Product Description

  • Soil: Clay or clay loam soil with good water retention and a pH range of 5.5–6.5 is ideal for rice cultivation. Ensure the soil has adequate drainage to prevent waterlogging during early growth stages.
  • Climate: Rice grows best in warm, humid climates with temperatures between 20–30°C. Consistent rainfall or a reliable irrigation system is crucial, especially during the vegetative and grain-filling stages.
  • Fertilizer: Apply organic fertilizers such as compost or farmyard manure before planting. Supplement with nitrogen-rich fertilizers every 20–25 days to encourage healthy growth.
  • Planting: Prepare the soil by ploughing and leveling it to ensure uniform water distribution. In flooded systems, create leveled fields for proper water management, or use rows spaced 20–25 cm apart in direct-seeded methods.
  • Transplanting: Transplant rice seedlings when they are about 20–25 days old. Water the field beforehand to ease planting, and ensure the seedlings are spaced about 15–20 cm apart for optimal growth.
  • Watering: Regular irrigation is essential for rice. Maintain a flooded field during the vegetative stage and a controlled water level during the grain-filling stage, then drain water 10–15 days before harvest.
  • Weeding: Begin weeding about 2–3 weeks after transplanting and continue as necessary, especially in the early growth stages, to minimize competition.
  • Harvesting: Rice is ready to harvest 110–150 days after planting, depending on the variety. Harvest when the grains turn golden and are firm.
  • Post-harvest Management: Thresh and clean the harvested rice to remove impurities. Store in a cool, dry, and ventilated area to prevent mold or pest damage.
