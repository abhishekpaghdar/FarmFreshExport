Ivy gourd

Product Description

  • Soil: Ivy gourd grows best in well-drained loamy or sandy loam soils rich in organic matter, with a pH range of 6.0–6.5. Adding compost or well-decomposed manure before planting can improve soil fertility.
  • Climate: Ivy gourd thrives in warm, tropical to subtropical climates with temperatures between 25–30°C. It requires full sunlight for optimal growth but is somewhat drought-tolerant once established.
  • Fertilizer: Apply organic fertilizer, such as compost or farmyard manure, before planting. Supplement with a balanced N-P-K fertilizer every 3–4 weeks during the growing season to support healthy growth and fruiting.
  • Planting: Ivy gourd is usually propagated from cuttings, as they grow faster and more reliably than seeds. Plant stem cuttings of about 20–30 cm in length, each with at least 3–4 nodes, directly in the soil. Space plants about 1 meter apart in rows spaced 2 meters apart.
  • Support: Ivy gourd is a climbing vine, so provide support using trellises, stakes, or a netting system to encourage vertical growth, better airflow, and easier harvesting.
  • Watering: Water regularly, especially during dry periods, to keep the soil consistently moist. Avoid overwatering, as ivy gourd does not tolerate waterlogged conditions well.
  • Weeding: Start weeding when the plants are young and continue as needed to reduce competition for nutrients and support healthy growth.
  • Harvesting: Ivy gourd is typically ready for harvest within 75–90 days after planting. Harvest the fruits when they are young, green, and tender, as they become harder and more bitter as they mature.
  • Post-harvest Management: Clean the harvested ivy gourds to remove soil or debris, and store them in a cool, dry place. Ivy gourd has a short shelf life, so consume or process soon after harvesting.
