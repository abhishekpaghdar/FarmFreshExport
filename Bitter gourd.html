Bitter gourd

Product Description

  • Soil: Bitter gourd grows best in well-drained, sandy loam or loamy soil rich in organic matter, witha pH range of 6.0–7.0. Adding compost or manure before planting can help improve soil fertility and moisture retention.
  • Climate: Bitter gourd thrives in warm, tropical to subtropical climates with temperatures between 24–30°C. It requires full sunlight and grows well during the warmer months.
  • Fertilizer: Apply organic fertilizers like compost or farmyard manure before planting. During growth, a balanced N-P-K fertilizer can be applied every 3–4 weeks to encourage healthy foliage and fruiting.
  • Planting: Prepare the soil by ploughing and leveling. Sow seeds directly into the soil or start them in a nursery tray, and transplant when seedlings have 2–3 true leaves. Space plants about 1 meter apart in rows spaced 1.5 meters apart to allow for vine growth.
  • Support: Since bitter gourd is a climbing vine, provide support with trellises or stakes for the plants to grow vertically. This improves airflow, sunlight exposure, and makes harvesting easier.
  • Watering: Water regularly to maintain consistent soil moisture, especially during flowering and fruiting. Avoid overwatering, as waterlogged conditions can cause root rot.
  • Weeding: Begin weeding when the plants are established, and continue periodically to reduce competition and support healthy growth.
  • Harvesting: Bitter gourd is typically ready for harvest 55–60 days after sowing. Harvest when the fruits are green and tender, as they become bitterer as they mature.
  • Post-harvest Management: Wash the harvested bitter gourds to remove soil or debris, and store them in a cool, dry place to retain freshness. Bitter gourd has a relatively short shelf life, so consume or process soon after harvesting.
