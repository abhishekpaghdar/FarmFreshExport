Brinjal

Product Description

  • Soil: Brinjal grows best in well-drained, loamy or sandy loam soil rich in organic matter, with a pH range of 6.0–7.0. Enrich the soil with compost or well-decomposed manure before planting to improve fertility and moisture retention.
  • Climate: Brinjal thrives in warm climates with temperatures between 25–30°C. It requires a sunny location and is sensitive to frost and cold temperatures, so plant it during warmer seasons or in regions with mild winters.
  • Fertilizer: Apply organic fertilizers, like compost or farmyard manure, before planting. During growth, use a balanced N-P-K fertilizer every 3–4 weeks to promote healthy foliage and fruit development.Like using sour buttermilk, neem oil, lemon etc.
  • Planting: Start seeds in a nursery or seed tray, and transplant seedlings when they are 6–8 weeks old, with 4–5 true leaves. Space plants about 45–60 cm apart in rows that are 75–90 cm apart for good airflow and access to sunlight.
  • Staking: Brinjal plants can grow tall and may need staking or support to keep the plants upright, especially during fruiting. This reduces the risk of plants bending or breaking under the weight of the fruits.
  • Watering: Water brinjal regularly to maintain consistent soil moisture, especially during flowering and fruiting stages. Avoid waterlogging, as brinjal roots are prone to rot.
  • Weeding: Begin weeding when the plants are established, and continue periodically to reduce competition for nutrients and promote better airflow around the plants.
  • Harvesting: Brinjal is typically ready for harvest 70–90 days after transplanting, depending on the variety. Harvest when the fruits are glossy, firm, and have reached their mature size. Overripe fruits may become bitter and tough.
  • Post-harvest Management: Handle harvested brinjal carefully to avoid bruising. Store them in a cool, dry place, but avoid refrigeration, as brinjal is sensitive to cold temperatures.
