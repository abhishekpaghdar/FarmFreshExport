Chilli

Product Description

  • Soil: Chili plants grow best in well-drained loamy soil with a pH range of 6.5–7.5. Enrich the soil with organic matter like compost or manure to improve fertility and moisture retention.
  • Climate: Chilis thrive in warm, humid climates with temperatures between 20–30°C. They require good sunlight but are sensitive to frost, so plant them in regions with mild winters.
  • Fertilizer: Apply organic fertilizers, such as compost or well-decomposed manure, before planting. Supplement with nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium-based fertilizers every 20–30 days to encourage flowering and fruiting.Like using sour buttermilk, neem oil, lemon etc.
  • Planting: Prepare the soil by ploughing and leveling it well. Sow seeds in a nursery or seed tray, then transplant the seedlings to the field when they are 4–6 weeks old. Space plants 45–60 cm apart in rows spaced 60–75 cm apart.
  • Transplanting: Transplant the seedlings in the evening to minimize transplant shock and water them immediately. Reduce watering one week before transplanting to harden the plants.
  • Watering: Water regularly to maintain soil moisture, especially during flowering and fruiting stages. Avoid overwatering, as chili plants are sensitive to waterlogging.
  • Weeding: Start weeding once the plants are established, and continue periodically to prevent competition for nutrients and improve airflow around the plants.
  • Harvesting: Chilis are typically ready for harvest 60–90 days after transplanting, depending on the variety. Harvest when the fruits are firm and glossy, either green or fully ripe.
  • Post-harvest Management: Dry harvested chilis in a shaded area to prevent sunburn and maintain color. Store in a cool, dry place to extend shelf life and prevent mold.
