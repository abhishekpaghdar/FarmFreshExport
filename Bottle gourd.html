Bottle gourd

Product Description

  • Soil: Bottle gourd grows best in well-drained, loamy soil rich in organic matter, with a pH range of 6.0–7.5. Adding compost or well-decomposed manure before planting improves soil fertility and moisture retention.
  • Climate: Bottle gourd thrives in warm, tropical to subtropical climates with temperatures between 25–35°C. It requires full sunlight and grows best during warm seasons.
  • Fertilizer: Apply organic fertilizers, like compost or farmyard manure, before planting. Supplement with a balanced N-P-K fertilizer every 3–4 weeks during growth to encourage healthy vines and fruiting.Like using sour buttermilk, neem oil, lemon etc.
  • Planting: Prepare the soil by ploughing and leveling it. Sow seeds directly in the soil or start them in a nursery. Plant seeds 2–3 cm deep, spaced 1–1.5 meters apart in rows that are 2 meters apart to allow ample room for vines to spread.
  • Support: Bottle gourd is a climbing vine, so provide a sturdy trellis or other support structure for the plants to grow vertically. This helps with sunlight exposure, airflow, and easier harvesting.
  • Watering: Water the plants regularly, especially during flowering and fruiting stages, to maintain consistent soil moisture. Avoid waterlogging, as bottle gourd roots are sensitive to rot.
  • Weeding: Begin weeding when the plants are young and continue periodically to reduce competition for nutrients and improve airflow around the plants.
  • Harvesting: Bottle gourd is usually ready for harvest 55–70 days after sowing. Harvest when the fruits are young, tender, and about 25–30 cm in length. Mature fruits can become hard and fibrous.
  • Post-harvest Management: Wash the harvested bottle gourds to remove soil or debris, and store them in a cool, dry place. Consume or process soon after harvesting to retain freshness.
