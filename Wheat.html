Wheat

Product Description

  • Soil: Wheat grows best in well-drained loam or clay loam soils with a pH range of 6.0–7.0. The soil should be rich in organic matter and nutrients for optimal growth.
  • Climate: Wheat thrives in cool, dry climates with temperatures between 10–25°C. It requires a dry climate for ripening, as excess moisture at this stage can affect grain quality.
  • Fertilizer: Apply organic fertilizers, such as compost, neem oil, biochar or well-rotted manure before planting. Supplement with nitrogen, phosphorus and potassium-based fertilizers as needed, especially during tillering and heading stages.
  • Planting: Prepare the soil by ploughing and leveling it well. Sow wheat seeds in rows with spacing of about 15–20 cm between them, ensuring seeds are sown 3–5 cm deep.
  • Transplanting: Wheat is usually direct-seeded rather than transplanted, so plant seeds directly into the prepared field.
  • Watering: Wheat requires moderate watering. Irrigate at key stages: tillering, jointing, heading, and grain filling. Avoid waterlogging, especially in the later stages.
  • Weeding: Begin weeding 2–3 weeks after planting and continue as necessary to reduce competition and improve yield.
  • Harvesting: Wheat is ready to harvest approximately 120–150 days after planting, depending on the variety. Harvest when the grains are firm and golden brown, and the stalks are dry.
  • Post-harvest Management: Thresh and clean the harvested wheat to remove chaff and other impurities. Store in a cool, dry, and ventilated area to prevent mold or insect infestation.
