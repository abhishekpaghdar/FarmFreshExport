Spiny gourd

Product Description

  • Soil: Spiny gourd grows best in well-drained, sandy loam or loamy soil rich in organic matter, with a pH range of 6.0–6.5. Adding compost or farmyard manure before planting can improve soil fertility and moisture retention.
  • Climate: Spiny gourd thrives in warm, tropical climates with temperatures between 25–30°C. It prefers areas with moderate rainfall and ample sunlight. The plant is drought-tolerant once established but benefits from regular watering during dry spells.
  • Fertilizer: Apply organic fertilizers, such as compost or well-decomposed manure, before planting. Supplement with a balanced N-P-K fertilizer every 3–4 weeks to encourage healthy growth and fruiting.Like using sour buttermilk, neem oil, lemon etc.
  • Planting: Spiny gourd is generally propagated through root tubers or cuttings, as it has a low germination rate from seeds. Plant tubers or cuttings in well-prepared soil, spaced about 1 meter apart in rows spaced 1.5–2 meters apart to allow for vine growth.
  • Support: Spiny gourd is a climbing vine, so provide support with trellises, stakes, or netting for vertical growth. This improves airflow, sunlight exposure, and makes harvesting easier.
  • Watering: Water regularly, especially during the early growth stage and during flowering and fruiting. Avoid overwatering, as spiny gourd does not tolerate waterlogged conditions well.
  • Weeding: Start weeding once the plants are established, and continue periodically to reduce competition and support healthy plant growth.
  • Harvesting: Spiny gourd fruits are typically ready to harvest 3–4 months after planting. Harvest when the fruits are small, green, and tender for the best flavor. Mature fruits become hard and bitter.
  • Post-harvest Management: Clean the harvested spiny gourds to remove soil or debris, and store them in a cool, dry place. Spiny gourd has a relatively short shelf life, so consume or process soon after harvesting.
