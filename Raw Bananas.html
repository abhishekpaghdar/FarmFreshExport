Raw Banana

Product Description

  • Soil: Well-drained, loamy soil with a pH range of 6.0–7.5 is ideal for cultivating raw bananas. Avoid waterlogged conditions to prevent root diseases.
  • Climate: Raw bananas thrive in warm, tropical climates with temperatures between 25–35°C. High humidity and moderate rainfall are ideal, but excessive moisture can cause rot.
  • Fertilizer: Apply organic fertilizers, such as compost or farmyard manure, every 20–25 days to maintain nutrient levels.
  • Planting: Prepare the soil by ploughing it thoroughly and incorporating organic manure. For banana cultivation, plant the suckers or tissue-cultured plants at a spacing of 2.5 x 2.5 meters.
  • Transplanting: Use healthy suckers or tissue-cultured plants for transplanting. It's best to plant them in the early morning or late afternoon for minimal transplant shock.
  • Watering: Bananas require consistent watering, particularly during the vegetative and fruiting stages, but avoid overwatering, as it may lead to root rot.
  • Weeding: Begin weeding after planting, and continue to keep the area weed-free to allow proper growth and reduce competition for nutrients.
  • Harvesting: Raw bananas are typically ready for harvest around 8–10 months after planting. Harvest when the fruit reaches full size but before full ripening, depending on the desired level of maturity.
  • Post-harvest Management: Clean the harvested bananas to remove soil or debris. Store in a cool, dry area to maintain quality and prevent post-harvest rot and pests.
