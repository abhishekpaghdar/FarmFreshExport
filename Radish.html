Radishes

Product Description

  • Soil: Radishes grow best in well-drained, loose, fertile soil rich in organic matter, with a pH range of 6.0–7.0. The soil should be free from rocks or hard clumps to allow the roots to grow freely. Add compost or well-decomposed manure to improve soil texture and fertility.
  • Climate: Radishes thrive in cooler climates with temperatures between 10–20°C. They can tolerate some heat, but excessively high temperatures may cause them to bolt (flower and go to seed) prematurely. Radishes are typically grown as a cool-season crop.
  • Fertilizer: Radishes are light feeders, but a small amount of compost or balanced organic fertilizer at planting time can promote healthy growth. Avoid excessive use of high-nitrogen fertilizers, as this can lead to poor root development.
  • Planting: Direct sow radish seeds into the soil 1–2 cm deep, spaced about 2–5 cm apart in rows that are 20–30 cm apart. For continuous harvesting, sow seeds every 2 weeks. Radishes grow quickly, with many varieties maturing in 25–40 days.
  • Watering: Radishes require consistent moisture, especially during the early growth stages, to develop proper root size. Water regularly to keep the soil moist but not soggy. Avoid letting the soil dry out, as this can cause the roots to become woody and develop a bitter taste.
  • Weeding: Weeding is essential early in the growing process to prevent competition for nutrients. Once established, radishes' foliage will help suppress weeds. Be careful when weeding to avoid damaging the tender roots.
  • Thinning: If seeds are sown too closely together, thin the seedlings to about 5–7 cm apart to allow enough space for the roots to grow properly. Thinning also helps reduce the risk of disease and pests.
  • Harvesting: Radishes are usually ready for harvest 25–40 days after sowing, depending on the variety. Harvest when the roots have reached their full size (about 2.5–5 cm in diameter). Radishes that stay in the ground too long may become tough and pithy, so it’s best to harvest them early.
  • Post-harvest Management: After harvesting, wash radishes thoroughly to remove soil. Store them in the refrigerator, where they can stay fresh for up to a week. Radishes can also be stored in a cool, dark place with high humidity for longer shelf life.
