Chilli

Product Description

  • Soil: Loamy soil with good drainage and a pH of 6.5–7.5 is ideal for growing chilli plants.
  • Climate: Chilli plants grow best in warm, humid climates with temperatures between 20–25°C. Heavy rainfall and high humidity can cause the fruit to rot.
  • Fertilizer: Apply organic fertilizer like compost slurry or banana peel fertilizer every 15–17 days.
  • Planting: Prepare the soil by ploughing it 2–3 times and adding compost or farm yard manure. Form ridges and furrows at a spacing of 60 cm.
  • Transplanting: Transplant seedlings when they are 4–8 weeks old. Transplant them at night and stop watering them one week before transplanting.
  • Watering: Timely irrigation is important, especially for fruit development and set.
  • Weeding: Begin weeding as soon as the seedlings are established.
  • Harvesting: The fruit is ready to harvest 40–50 days after germination. Post-harvest management Ensure the harvested materials are washed and cleaned to remove soil particles. Store the produce in a clean, dry, and undamaged area to prevent insect pests.
