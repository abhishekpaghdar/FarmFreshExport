Turmeric

Product Description

  • Soil: Turmeric grows best in well-drained, loamy or sandy loam soil rich in organic matter, with a pH range of 5.5–7.0. Adding compost or well-decomposed manure before planting helps improve soil fertility and moisture retention.
  • Climate: Turmeric thrives in warm, humid tropical climates with temperatures between 20–30°C. It requires 7–10 months of frost-free, warm weather and consistent rainfall or irrigation for optimal growth.
  • Fertilizer: Apply organic fertilizers, such as compost or well-decomposed farmyard manure, before planting. Supplement with balanced N-P-K fertilizer every 2–3 months to support strong rhizome development.
  • Planting: Turmeric is propagated through rhizomes, not seeds. Cut rhizomes into 2.5–5 cm pieces, each with at least one or two buds. Plant the rhizome pieces about 5–7 cm deep, spaced 20–30 cm apart in rows that are 45–60 cm apart.
  • Mulching: After planting, cover the soil with organic mulch (such as straw or leaves) to retain moisture, suppress weeds, and improve soil temperature.
  • Watering: Turmeric requires consistent moisture, especially during the early growth stage and the rhizome development phase. Water regularly but avoid waterlogging, as it can cause rhizome rot.
  • Weeding: Begin weeding once the plants are established, and continue as needed throughout the growing season. Turmeric benefits from weed-free soil for optimal growth.
  • Harvesting: Turmeric is ready for harvest 8–10 months after planting, typically when the leaves and stems start to yellow and dry out. Carefully dig up the rhizomes and separate them from the plant.
  • Post-harvest Management: Wash the harvested rhizomes thoroughly to remove soil. Boil them for 45–60 minutes, then sun-dry them for 10–15 days or until fully dried. Store dried rhizomes in a cool, dry place, or process them into turmeric powder.
