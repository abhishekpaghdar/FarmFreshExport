Glue Berry

Product Description

  • Soil: Lasora grows well in well-drained loamy or sandy loam soils with a pH range of 6.5–7.5. The soil should have good moisture retention, so adding organic matter like compost or manure can help improve fertility.
  • Climate: Lasora thrives in warm, tropical to subtropical climates with temperatures between 20–35°C. It is drought-resistant but performs best in areas with moderate rainfall. The plant is tolerant of semi-arid conditions and requires good sunlight.
  • Fertilizer: Apply organic fertilizers, such as compost or farmyard manure, before planting. An annual application of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium-based fertilizers supports growth and fruit production.
  • Planting: Lasora is usually grown from seeds or stem cuttings. Sow seeds or plant cuttings at a depth of 2–3 cm in rows spaced 5–6 meters apart, as the tree can grow large and requires ample space.
  • Watering: Young plants need regular watering to establish roots, especially during dry periods. Once established, lasora is fairly drought-resistant but benefits from periodic watering during flowering and fruiting.
  • Weeding: Begin weeding as soon as the plants are established, and continue periodically to reduce competition and support healthy growth.
  • Harvesting: Lasora fruits are usually ready to harvest 6–8 months after flowering. Pick the fruits when they are green or slightly yellow, depending on the desired ripeness and use (fresh or processed).
  • Post-harvest Management: After harvesting, wash the fruits to remove any soil or debris. Dry them properly before storing in a cool, dry place to prevent spoilage.
