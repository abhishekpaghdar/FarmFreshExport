Peas

Product Description

  • Sow organic seed 4 to 6 weeks before the last spring frost date when the soil is cool, or when it is at the desired temperature. Peas planted in cool (40°F) soil will germinate slowly; Peas planted in soil that is at least 60°F (but not more than 85°F) will germinate. Snow will not harm emerging pea plants, but several days with temperatures in the teens may occur. Be prepared to replant if the first peas don't make it. Alternatively, try starting your peas in a cold frame.
  • Water peas no more than 1 inch per week, unless the plant is dry. We don't want to promote pea rot. But don't let the plant dry out. If this happens, no pods will be produced.
  • Spraying substances like lemon oil, jaggery and insecticides to kill pests and diseases. Gently remove stubborn weeds by hand. Hoe or cultivate if necessary, but carefully avoid disturbing the pea's shallow, delicate roots.
  • Most pea varieties are ready for harvest 60 to 70 days after planting. Peas mature quickly, so check daily once you see the flowers blooming.
  • Store the peas in the refrigerator for about five days. Place in a paper bag, then wrap in plastic.
