Tomato

Product Description

  • Soil: Tomatoes thrive in well-drained, loamy soil rich in organic matter, with a pH range of 6.0–6.8. Adding compost or well-rotted manure before planting improves fertility and moisture retention.
  • Climate: Tomatoes grow best in warm, sunny conditions with temperatures between 20–25°C. They are sensitive to frost and require consistent warmth, so plant them in regions with mild climates or during warmer seasons.
  • Fertilizer: Apply organic fertilizer like compost or farmyard manure before planting. During growth, a balanced N-P-K fertilizer can be applied every 3–4 weeks, especially during the flowering and fruiting stages, to support healthy growth.
  • Planting: Sow seeds in a nursery tray, and transplant the seedlings when they are 4–6 weeks old and have at least 2–3 true leaves. Space plants 45–60 cm apart in rows that are 75–100 cm apart to ensure enough airflow and light exposure.
  • Staking: Tomato plants need support as they grow, so use stakes, cages, or trellises to keep the plants upright. This improves air circulation, reduces disease risk, and makes harvesting easier.
  • Watering: Water tomatoes regularly to keep the soil consistently moist, especially during flowering and fruiting. Avoid overhead watering to prevent fungal diseases; drip irrigation or watering at the base of the plants is ideal.
  • Weeding: Begin weeding once the plants are established, and continue regularly to reduce competition and improve air circulation around the plants.
  • Harvesting: Tomatoes are typically ready for harvest 60–90 days after transplanting, depending on the variety. Harvest them when they are fully colored (red, yellow, or other colors depending on the variety) but still firm.
  • Post-harvest Management: Handle tomatoes carefully to avoid bruising. Store harvested tomatoes in a cool, dry place to maintain freshness and prevent spoilage.
